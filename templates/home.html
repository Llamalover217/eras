Different Classical Eras and Timeline:

- Greek (700-323BCE)
- Hellenistic (323-146BCE)
- Roman (44BCE-476CE)

A bit about the Classical Era:

The Classical Era is derived from the architecture of Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome. It was constructed in the 5th century BC in Greece and in the 3rd cenury AD in Rome. The era emphasised the column and pediment and evolved from timber origins. The style was revived many times over the years. During the Italin renaissance, architects worked to restore Classical Roman Architecture.

Characteristics of the Classical Era:

- Symmetry
- Columns
- Rectangular Windows
- Marble or some other attractive, durable stone

Classical Architecture Architects

Vitruvius (80BC-15BC)
--> Full name: Marcus Vitruvius Pollio
--> Roman author, architect, and civil and military engineer

Sebastiano Serlio (1475-1554)
--> Italian Mannerist architect
--> Was part of the Italian team building the Palace of Fontainebleau
--> Helped canonise the classical orders of architecture

Andrea Palladio (1508-1580)
--> Italian architect
--> Influenced by Roman and Greek architecture, primarily Vitruvius

Vincenzo Scamozzi (1548-1616)
--> Italian architect
--> Active mainly in Vicenza and Republic of Venice area in the second half of the 16th century

Robert Adam (1728-1792)
--> Scottish neoclassical architect, interior designer and furniture designer
--> Son of William Adam, Scotland’s foremost architect of all time

John Soane (1753-1837)
--> Specialised in Neo-Classical style
--> Became a professor of architecture at the Royal Academy
--> An official architect to the Office of Works
--> Received knighthood in 1831

Georges Gromort (1870-1961)
--> French architect
--> Wrote a book called ‘The elements of classical architecture’

Chester Holmes Aldrich (1871-1940)
--> American architect
--> Director of the American Academy in Rome

John Russell Pope (1874-1937)
--> American architect
--> His firm is widely known for designing major public buildings including the National Archives and Records Administration building

Allan Greenberg (1938-present)
--> American architect
--> One of the leading classical architects of the twenty-first century - also known as New Classical Architecture

Léon Krier (1946-present)
--> Luxembourgish architect, architectural theorist and urban planner
--> Combines an international architecture and planning practice with writing and teaching

Peter Pennoyer (1957-present)
--> Architect and principal of Peter Pennoyer Architects in New York City
--> Has an international practice in traditional and classical architecture, or New Classical Architecture

Duncan G. Stroik (1962-present)
--> American architect
--> Professor of Architecture at the University of Notre Dame School of Architecture
